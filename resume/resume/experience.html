Rhetorical Analysis of Text-to-Image Models

Study Overview: To understand the ethics of the messages communicated by these text-to-image models, this study analyzed the messages communicated by 2,050 images generated by the model. Specifically, the rhetorical analysis considered whether the messages reinforced gender biases and how those gender biases impact the amount of rhetorical agency that we should grant these text-to-image models. In other words, the rhetorical analysis aimed to understand to what levels we should rely on text-to-image models to produce the images that we use.

Research Question: “What messages do the images’ algorithmic biases produce related to gender and how do these messages impact the levels of rhetorical agency that we grant text-to-image models?”

Read the report.

Qualitative Study on AI-Generated Communication

Project Overview: This study served as a textual analysis of a human-produced writing by Google’s Chief Diversity Officer to inform the general public of Google’s diversity and inclusion initiatives and an AI-produced writing with the same intended audience, purpose, and length that could, theoretically, replace Google’s article. The study implemented the APA guidelines for bias-free and inclusive language as its criteria.

Research Question: “When writing technical communication, how do the amounts and types of biases in pieces written by the Artificial Intelligence chatbot ChatGPT compare to those in pieces written by a human?”
